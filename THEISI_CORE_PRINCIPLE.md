# THEISI — CORE PRINCIPLE
## The one idea everything checks against
### v1.0 · 2026-06-06 · Authored with Rashed

> This document sits **above** the SSOT and the Brand skill. The SSOT says *how the
> system works*. The Brand skill says *how it looks and sounds*. This document says
> **why THEISI exists and what it refuses to become.** When any build decision —
> dashboard, Instagram, Telegram, website, AI prompts — is unclear, it is resolved

## 1. THE MISSION, IN ONE SENTENCE

**We gather the scattered data and connect the dots — so you see what matters,
and decide for yourself.**

Arabic: **نجمع البيانات ونربط بين النقاط — لتُدرك ما يهم، وتُقرّر بثقة.**

The mission is a chain, and each link matters:

> **Gather** the scattered data → **connect the dots** (organize it, show how the
> pieces affect each other) → the user **sees** the whole clear picture → **they decide.**

We bring all the data to you, organized and explained. You don't jump around ten tabs
and sources to collect it — we did that, and we connected the dots between the pieces.
But we stop before the final dot. We make the picture clear; **you** draw the conclusion.

Tagline (unchanged): **افهم ما يهم — Understand What Matters.**

### The name is the mission
**Connect the dots → so They See → THEISI.**
The brand name *is* the outcome of the work. THEISI ("They See") is what happens
*after* we connect the dots: the user sees clearly. The act (connecting the dots) and
the outcome (they see) are the whole brand in three words. We connect the dots **so
they see** — never so we decide for them.

## 2. WHAT WE ARE — AND ARE NOT

| THEISI IS | THEISI IS NOT |
|-----------|---------------|
| A data-gathering and organizing service | A financial advisor |
| An explainer of how signals interact | A recommendation engine |
| A single calm place to read the whole picture | A "buy now" alert machine |
| Honest about uncertainty and margin of error | A source of false confidence |
| The legwork done for you | The decision made for you |

**We never say "buy this" or "sell this."**
We say: *"Here is everything. Here is what each number means. Here is where the
signals agree and where they pull against each other. Now you decide."*

---

## 3. THE LANGUAGE RULE (applies to ALL surfaces)

Every derived number, score, or signal is framed as **a sense of direction — a feeling
of where things lean — never as fact or instruction.**

### Forbidden tone (never do this)
- "Conviction: 82% — BUY ✓"
- "Strong Buy signal"
- "You should add this position"
- Any number presented as hard truth with no margin of error

### Required tone (always do this)
- "The signals **lean positive** this week — a sense of direction, not a verdict."
- "This is calculated from X + Y + Z. It has a **wide margin of error.**"
- "PEG says cheap, but price sits above targets — these **pull in opposite
  directions**, worth sitting with."
- "We gather and explain. **The decision is yours.**"

**Every derived metric must carry, nearby:**
1. **How** it was calculated (the inputs and weights)
2. **What** it means in plain language
3. **The caveat** — that it's directional, soft, and carries a large margin of error

## 4. THE DERIVED METRICS — HOW THEY ARE FRAMED

These are THEISI's own composites, not published facts. They exist to **summarize and
compare quickly**, never to instruct.

### Signal Read / قراءة الإشارات (the conviction gauge)
- **Calc:** Consensus (30%) + Upside Potential (40%) + Analyst Grades (30%)
- **Framed as:** "leaning positive / neutral / leaning cautious" — تميل إيجابياً / محايدة / تميل بحذر
- **Caveat:** "A sense of where the signals lean this week. Not a recommendation.
  Wide margin of error."

### Risk / Volatility Read / مستوى التقلّب
- **Calc:** PEG + recent volatility (standard deviation)
- **Framed as:** Low / Medium / High — منخفض / متوسط / مرتفع
- **Caveat:** "A rough sense of how bumpy this might be — not a full risk assessment.
  Ignores debt, sector cycles, liquidity."

### Tension callout / إشارات متضاربة
- When two signals disagree (e.g. cheap PEG vs price above target), **say so explicitly.**
- This is core: we surface the conflict instead of hiding it behind a single score.
- "These pull in opposite directions — worth contemplating."

## 5. WHERE THIS LIVES (audit every surface against §3 and §4)

This principle is not decoration on one screen. It is audited across **every** surface:

- **Dashboard** — Stock Lookup, AI Advisor, Scoring, Fair Value, every derived number
  carries its how/what/caveat. The disclaimer stays visible on results.
- **Telegram** — the morning brief and analysis explain and contextualize; they never
  issue commands. Soft directional language only.
- **Instagram** — content educates and explains "what matters and why," never "buy X."
  No hype, no false certainty.
- **Website** — the methodology page exists to *prove* this honesty. It explains the
  5-layer engine in plain language: "we check X because Y." The product promise on the
  home page is the mission from §1.
- **AI prompts (Make.com 255 / 357)** — system prompts instruct the model to gather,
  organize, explain, surface tensions, and **decline to give direct buy/sell commands.**
  The 357 (per-user) tier stays informational-only with disclaimer, for legal reasons.

These pair with the existing tagline (افهم ما يهم) as the expanded promise. The phrase

brand name (They See).

## 7. THE AUDIT CHECKLIST (run before shipping anything)

Before any dashboard change, post, message, or page goes live, check:

- [ ] Does any number pretend to be a fact when it's actually a derived estimate?
- [ ] Does every derived score show how it's calculated and what it means?
- [ ] Is there a visible caveat / margin-of-error note near soft metrics?
- [ ] Does the language describe a *direction/lean*, never an *instruction*?
- [ ] Are conflicting signals surfaced honestly, not smoothed into one verdict?
- [ ] Is the "we organize & explain, you decide" framing present?
- [ ] Is the legal disclaimer visible where decisions could be influenced?
- [ ] Would a careful user feel *more* trust because we showed our work — not less?

If any box is unchecked, it is not ready.

---

## 8. WHY THIS PROTECTS US

Showing the methodology and admitting uncertainty is not a weakness — it is the moat.
Anyone can show a green "BUY" badge. Almost no one in Arabic financial media does the
honest, patient work of laying out the whole picture and trusting the reader to think.
That honesty **is** the product. It is also what keeps us on the right side of the line
between "information service" and "unlicensed financial advice."

**When in doubt: gather, organize, explain, and step back. The decision is theirs.**
